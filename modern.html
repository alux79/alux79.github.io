Tension from Modern Education

Contrasting Ideas

Cambodia’s education system is quickly modernizing through new teaching strategies, curriculum reforms, international influences, and increased access to improving technology. Through these changes, Cambodia attempts to be seen in the scope of the world. Heng and Koemhong (2022) highlight that the modernization of education is imperative for Cambodia’s competitiveness in the world emphasizing that reforms should focus on values like critical thinking and innovation. These however, greatly contrast traditional Cambodian values and often inhibit things like character and morality (Chaika 2021). This then brings the question, how far do these reforms need to go before we lose something greater?

Modern Values in Conflict

Modern View: Encourages students to analyze, question, and evaluate information independently. Knowledge is considered dynamic and open to exploration.

Conflict: Questioning authority figures like teachers can be seen as disrespectful or rebellious, challenging traditional classroom hierarchies.

Modern View: Values personal identity, creativity, and diverse viewpoints, encouraging students to confidently voice their unique perspectives.

Conflict: Traditional values emphasize community harmony and collective interests, potentially viewing strong individual expression as disruptive or self-centered.

Modern View: Focuses on adapting instruction to each learner’s specific needs, empowering students with greater agency over their own education.

Conflict: Traditional Cambodian classrooms prioritize structured authority and discipline, making this shift toward learner autonomy challenging and potentially uncomfortable.

Modern View: Promotes learning through exploration, curiosity-driven questioning, and practical, real-world problem-solving.

Conflict: Traditional Cambodian education emphasizes memorization, repetition, and clearly defined outcomes, viewing open-ended questioning as inefficient or unclear.

Modern View: Actively embraces technological advancements, creative problem-solving, and continual adaptation to new ideas and approaches.

Conflict: Traditional education values stability, predictability, and established methods, leading to perceptions that innovation is risky or disconnected from cultural heritage.

What to do?

The tension between modern and traditional educational values create a complex scene. Modern systems, as Heng and Koemhong (2022) and Sen (2022) argue, very clearly provide students with vital skills such as critical thinking, individual expression, and innovation that are truly important in the modernizing world, yet these ideas undermine traditional Cambodian values, potentially weakening ideas of social cohesion, discipline, and respect (Purwaningsih & Ridha, 2024). Furthermore, traditional values still are an important aspect of growing community, stability, and a strong identity. This results in a difficult question. If modern reforms directly contrast with traditional values, how is it possible to evolve the educational system without losing important cultural elements, while also using the beneficial parts of traditional values?
